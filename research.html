Research Summary

My research has focused on better understanding air pollution and its impacts on human health & climate change.

Toward that end, I have carried out many studies of air pollution (mainly aerosols) in UK, Spain, Italy, Czech, South Korea and currently in China and India. I worked in two great EU-H2020 projects: “Human exposure to aerosol contaminants in modern microenvironments”, and “Source apportionment of airport emissions in the context of European Air Quality Directives”. My current project concerns on Atmospheric Pollution & Human Health in a Chinese Megacity (a joint UK-China programme, c.a. £12 mil funded by NERC-MRC-NSFC Newton Fund). These projects have provided me with considerable experience with regards to air pollution and numerical modelling.

In addition, I have successfully developed and applied the source apportionment and lung deposition models to identify which source is responsible for aerosols deposited in the human respiratory system. I has recently applied a novel machine learning to evaluate the effectiveness of the "Air Pollution Prevention and Control Action Plan" on the Beijing air quality.

My publication list can also be found on the Google Scholar website
