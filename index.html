Tuan V. Vu

I am a Research Fellow in Atmospheric Science (in Prof. Roy Harrison's group) in University of Birmingham, UK.

My research interests focus on sources and health effects of air pollution. I am also interested in numerical analysis, statistics and machine learning for air quality modelling.

I have carried out many studies of air pollution (mainly aerosols) in UK, Spain, Italy, Czech, South Korea and currently in China and India. I worked in two great EU-H2020 projects: “Human exposure to aerosol contaminants in modern microenvironments”, and “Source apportionment of airport emissions in the context of European Air Quality Directives”. My current project concerns on Atmospheric Pollution & Human Health in a Chinese Megacity (a joint UK-China programme, c.a. £12 mil funded by NERC-MRC-NSFC Newton Fund). These projects have provided me with considerable experience with regards to air pollution and numerical modelling. I have successfully developed and applied the source apportionment and lung deposition models to identify which source is responsible for aerosols deposited in the human respiratory system. I has recently applied a novel machine learning to evaluate the effectiveness of the "Air Pollution Prevention and Control Action Plan" on the Beijing air quality.
